Advanced


      
Reader

      
      

        Controls whether the reader offers to load a previously cached
        or relay-published copy of the page. "Always" surfaces any
        non-identical archive; "Only when richer" preserves the older
        paywall-shaped heuristic.
      


      

      
Workspace

      

        A workspace is everything you have captured or authored:
        entities (and their keypairs, including the entity-sync key),
        claims, evidence links, assessments, forensic findings, truth
        adjudications, platform accounts, the archive cache, audit
        records, and the signed-event journal. Settings, relays,
        feature flags, the LLM key, and your saved
        identities are not part of it.
      

      

        
        
      

      

        The backup contains your saved identities and their private
        keys — treat the file like an nsec. Audit records cost money to
        recompute; the reset flow offers the backup first.
      

      

      

      
Full backup

      

        Everything the extension stores locally in one file: the workspace
        above plus settings, saved identities, the captured
        article archive, original source documents, audit records, and the
        signed-event journal. Restoring it on a fresh install brings the
        whole extension back. It contains private keys — treat the file
        like an nsec. Your LLM API key is never included.
      

      
      

        
        
        
      

      

        Restore replaces everything with the backup's
        contents — a safety backup of the current data downloads first.
        The signed-events bundle is the raw signed JSON of every event you
        have published (no keys inside) — anyone can rebroadcast it to any
        relay, so your corpus survives a relay outage.
      

      
      

      

      
Experimental


      
      

        Off by default. When on, the reader's Publish batch also emits your
        judgments — kind-30054 assessments (stance + labels + rationale),
        kind-30055 claim links, and a one-time kind-1985 label mirror per
        labeled assessment — signed by your publishing identity and
        publicly visible to anyone with relay access.
        Local capture always works regardless of this switch.
      


      

      
Epistemic audits

      
      

        Off by default. When on, the reader's Publish batch also emits
        the published article's imported audit results (kinds
        30056/30057), its prediction ledger entries (30058), and your
        resolutions of its predictions (30059) — scoped to that one
        article per publish, signed by your publishing identity, and
        publicly visible to anyone with relay access.
        A published low score is a public, attributed, disputable
        record. Local import and rendering never need this switch.
      

      

        Run the companion scorer CLI (docs/auditor-prototype/scorer/)
        against a captured article, then import its JSON here. Imports
        are re-hashed and schema-validated, and must match a local
        capture — the audit has to be about text you actually captured.
        Importing stores results locally only; publishing audit events
        is a separate, experimental-flagged step.
      

      

        
        
        
      

      

      

      
Forensic findings

      
      

        Off by default. When on, the reader's Publish batch also emits your
        behavioral findings — kind-30062 (the named maneuver + its evidence
        chain + a required counter-read), a one-time kind-1985 maneuver
        mirror, and any revision/* story-change edges
        (kind-30055) — signed by your publishing identity and
        publicly visible to anyone with relay access. A
        finding names a structure, not a verdict; it labels the
        subject's pubkey, so it only publishes for a subject you've tagged
        as a keyed entity. Local capture always works regardless.
      


      

      
Truth adjudication

      
      

        Off by default. When on, the reader's Publish batch also emits your
        adjudicated verdicts — kind-30063 (a descriptive ruling on a
        proposition, with its declared standard of proof, verbatim evidence,
        and required caveats) plus a one-time kind-1985 mirror labeling the
        claim — and your integrity findings (kind-30064, a subject's stated
        words vs enacted deeds), signed by your publishing identity and
        publicly visible to anyone with relay access. A
        verdict attaches to a proposition, never a person; an
        integrity finding labels the subject's pubkey, so it only publishes
        for a subject tagged as a keyed entity, and only once every claim
        it references is itself published. Local adjudication (the reader's
        🏛 flow) always works regardless.
      


      

      
Entity corpus

      
      

        Off by default. When on, the reader's Publish batch assembles each
        tagged entity's dossier and emits an enriched kind-0 profile (its
        about built only from facts whose claims are themselves
        published — every line says "per <source>", and contested
        fields are omitted entirely) plus a kind-30067 fact sheet (the full
        field table, contested fields shown both sides, every fact
        referencing its published claim). These are signed by the
        entity's own keys and publicly visible to anyone with relay
        access; once relayed they are irrevocable in practice —
        NIP-09 deletion requests are best-effort only and many relays
        ignore them. Re-publishes happen automatically only when a
        profile's content actually changed. Local dossiers always work
        regardless.
      


      

      
Identity sharing

      
      

        Off by default. When on, the reader's Publish batch also emits a
        kind-32126 record for each platform account this capture touched
        (post author, commenters) and each account linked to a tagged
        entity — the cross-user rendezvous: anyone can then discover your
        capture of an account and which entity you say it belongs
        to. Your captured-account → entity links become
        public. For adversarial casework, prefer per-case
        identities (Signing tab).
      


      

      
LLM assist

      
      

        Off by default. When on and an API key is set, the
        reader gains a ✨ Suggest… control that asks Anthropic's
        Claude to propose capture artifacts (choose which types below) from the
        open article. The article text is sent to Anthropic
        when you run a pass. Every suggestion is a draft you review and
        confirm — nothing is saved or published automatically, and
        publishing still requires the experimental publish switches above.

        export, and never logged.

Moral lens

      
      

        Off by default, and independent of LLM assist above. When on
        and an Anthropic API key is set (same key field as
        LLM assist), the reader gains a Lens readings bar
        that reads selected claims under jurisdictions you author yourself —
        none ship built-in. A lens pass sends the article text
        plus the jurisdiction definitions and captured authority
        excerpts to Anthropic — one API call per empaneled
        jurisdiction. Readings are perspectival reconstructions grounded in
        each jurisdiction's own loaded corpus; they never say whether
        anything is true (that stays with the truth layer). Nothing is
        durably saved and nothing is published — results live only for the
        browser session.
      


      

      
Power user


      
      

        Off by default. Turning this on produces ~15 lines per event in the
        browser console — useful for diagnosing publish failures, noisy for
        everyday use.
